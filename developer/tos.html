Developer Policies

Last updated: 29 January 2026

Introduction

These Developer Policies govern how CTK SDKs, libraries, APIs, and developer tools may be used. By integrating, distributing, or executing any CTK-provided code, you agree to comply with these Policies.

CTK SDK / Libraries
Any official CTK-distributed code, binaries, APIs, or tooling.
Developer Content
Any application or project that uses CTK SDKs or libraries.
Enforcement Code
Logic that restricts or disables access based on policy violations.
CTK Blacklist
A permanent or temporary restriction applied to users, accounts, or IPs.
Section A

Acceptable SDK & Library Usage

  1. Authorized Use Only CTK SDKs may only be used in accordance with their documented purpose. Reverse engineering, deobfuscation, or bypassing built-in safeguards is prohibited.
    Immediate SDK revocation + CTK Blacklist
  2. No Repackaging or Redistribution You may not repackage, resell, sublicense, or redistribute CTK SDKs without explicit written authorization.
    Permanent CTK Blacklist + potential legal action
  3. Content Responsibility Developers are solely responsible for applications built using CTK tools. CTK may restrict SDK functionality if used to distribute harmful or illegal content.
    Feature restriction or full SDK disablement
  4. Security & Integrity Attempts to interfere with CTK infrastructure, authentication, telemetry, or enforcement mechanisms are strictly prohibited.
    Automatic enforcement triggers + IP blocking + CTK Blacklist
Section B

Blacklisting & Runtime Enforcement

  1. Execution-Level Restrictions CTK reserves the right to execute server-side or client-side enforcement code that may limit, disable, or fully block functionality for blacklisted users or environments.
  2. No Circumvention Any attempt to bypass CTK enforcement logic, blacklist checks, or license validation is a severe violation.
    Permanent CTK Blacklist
  3. Propagation of Restrictions Blacklist status may apply across all CTK services, SDKs, and partner integrations. Enforcement may occur automatically without prior notice.
  4. Finality Blacklist decisions are generally final. CTK is not obligated to provide appeals, explanations, or reinstatement.
Section C

Developer & Administrative Misconduct

  1. Abuse of Privileged Access Developers or administrators with elevated access may not misuse credentials, keys, or permissions to alter CTK systems or harm other developers.
    Immediate access revocation + CTK Blacklist + potential legal action
  2. Intentional System Damage Any deliberate attempt to disrupt, degrade, or destroy CTK services or SDK functionality is a critical violation.
    Permanent CTK Blacklist + possible civil or criminal escalation
Section D

Telemetry, Monitoring & Privacy

CTK SDKs may collect limited telemetry required for security, enforcement, and stability. Use of CTK tools constitutes consent to this monitoring as outlined in our Privacy Policy.

Section E

Policy Updates

CTK may update these Developer Policies at any time. Continued use of CTK SDKs or libraries after an update constitutes acceptance of the revised Policies.
